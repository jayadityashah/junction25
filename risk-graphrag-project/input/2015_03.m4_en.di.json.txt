PARAGRAPH_25321: 3 Objectives

[JURISDICTION:FINNISH] [SOURCE:FIVA_MOK] [CATEGORY:FINANCIAL_REGULATION] [PAGE:7]
PARAGRAPH_25322: (1) The LTV ratio is a macroprudential tool, which can be used to contain excessive household indebtedness and prevent a spiral of credit expansion and inflation of market prices of assets used as collateral. This macroprudential tool is particularly appropriate for containing any overheating of the housing markets.

[JURISDICTION:FINNISH] [SOURCE:FIVA_MOK] [CATEGORY:FINANCIAL_REGULATION] [PAGE:7]
PARAGRAPH_25323: (2) Other objectives of the regulation are

[JURISDICTION:FINNISH] [SOURCE:FIVA_MOK] [CATEGORY:FINANCIAL_REGULATION] [PAGE:7]
PARAGRAPH_25324: · to promote advance saving for a home; and

[JURISDICTION:FINNISH] [SOURCE:FIVA_MOK] [CATEGORY:FINANCIAL_REGULATION] [PAGE:7]
PARAGRAPH_25325: · to prevent situations where the debt of a housing loan customer exceeds the value of the collateral in the event of a potential decrease of house prices (negative equity).

[JURISDICTION:FINNISH] [SOURCE:FIVA_MOK] [CATEGORY:FINANCIAL_REGULATION] [PAGE:7]
PARAGRAPH_25326: (3) The objective of these regulations and guidelines is to describe the calculation of the loan-to- value ratio as referred to in chapter 15, section 11 of the Act on Credit Institutions and section 14 of the Act on the Registration of Certain Credit Providers and Credit Intermediaries in Finland, and thereby harmonise the concept of LTV in the market of housing loans for personal customers. (Issued on 24.5.2023, valid from 1.7.2023)
