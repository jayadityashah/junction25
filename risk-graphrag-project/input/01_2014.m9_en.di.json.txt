PARAGRAPH_29075: 3.1 Objectives of the regulations and guidelines

[JURISDICTION:FINNISH] [SOURCE:FIVA_MOK] [CATEGORY:FINANCIAL_REGULATION] [PAGE:6] [RISK:CREDIT_RISK]
PARAGRAPH_29076: (1) These regulations and guidelines define supervised entities' reporting obligations in respect of credit risk and the reporting of loan-to-value ratio.

[JURISDICTION:FINNISH] [SOURCE:FIVA_MOK] [CATEGORY:FINANCIAL_REGULATION] [PAGE:6] [RISK:CREDIT_RISK] [REQ_TYPE:MANDATORY]
PARAGRAPH_29077: (2) The objective of these regulations and guidelines is to ensure that FIN-FSA can acquire information on supervised entities' credit risk position and the loan-to-value ratios of housing loans granted by supervised entities. Provisions are necessary to protect the public e.g. from the risk of losing repayable funds and to safeguard the reliability and stability of the banking system.

[JURISDICTION:FINNISH] [SOURCE:FIVA_MOK] [CATEGORY:FINANCIAL_REGULATION] [PAGE:6] [RISK:CREDIT_RISK]
PARAGRAPH_29078: (3) Credit risks are significant risks to which supervised entities with banking operations are exposed. Supervised entities' credit risk management and control are of major importance for ensuring capital adequacy in relation to operational risks and any losses resulting from materialisation of such risks.

[JURISDICTION:FINNISH] [SOURCE:FIVA_MOK] [CATEGORY:FINANCIAL_REGULATION] [PAGE:6] [REQ_TYPE:MANDATORY]
PARAGRAPH_29079: (4) The objective of reporting on the loan-to-value ratio is to ensure that FIN-FSA can acquire information on the loan-to-value ratios of residential mortgage loans granted by supervised entities. The Credit Institutions Act requires that supervised entities not grant residential mortgage loans larger than as prescribed in chapter 15, section 11 of the Credit Institutions Act.
